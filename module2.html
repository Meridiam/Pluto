Module 2:

Avoiding Fraud

Fraudsters use these tactics
The Phantom Riches tactic: dangling the prospect of wealth, enticing you with something you want but have.

For example: "Investing in these gold stocks will yield $10,000 a month in income."

What is the "Phantom Riches" tactic?

The Source Credibility Tactic: trying to build credibility by claiming to be with a reputable firm or to have special credentials or experience.

For example: "As a former head representative of XYZ firm, I would never market a try to sell an investment that will underperform."

The Social Consensus Tactic: leading you to believe that other savvy investors have already invested.

For example: "This is how (insert rich investor's name here) got his to where he began today"

The Reciprocity Tactic: offering to do a small favor for you in return for a big favor.

For example: "I won't consider my commission and allow you purchase right now 75% off."

The Scarcity Tactic: creating a false sense of urgency by claiming limited supply.

For example: "There are only two units left, buy quickly before they run out."
