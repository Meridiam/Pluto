Investment 101

Short term investor: looking to make a quick buck stuff you want to look at the short term market price is going up

Long term investor: Look if the overall trend for the stock is going up

Conservative or retiring: Look at how much interest your stock provides

Overall tips: Calculate overall return Factor in inflation Consider taxes on profits Check an index for that particular stock(include an animation for Dow Jones here)
Selling a long term investment, an investment purchased for over a year, will be taxed at a reduced rate while shor term investments will be taxed at your income rate
Save money with asset allocation and diversifying and rebalancing your portfolio
For asset allocation: you decide how much money you put into for different types of investments
For diversifying your portfolio: you decide inside the asset to invest in several different sub groups
For rebalancing your portfolio: once a stock grows to quickly it is wise to reallocate investments to a slower growing investment because growing investments are more susceptible to crashes
Realize investing is like gambling: Gains are not guarenteed there are risks involved and you can lose money you can mitigate these risks by reallocating assets or diversifying your your portfolio
